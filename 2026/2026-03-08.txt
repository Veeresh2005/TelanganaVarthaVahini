## Telangana News Bulletin: March 8, 2026

### 1. Political

The Congress government, led by Chief Minister Revanth Reddy, completed its second anniversary and three months in office, facing intense scrutiny from the opposition BRS and BJP regarding the pace of welfare scheme implementation and the state's financial health. Political analysts point towards the upcoming Greater Hyderabad Municipal Corporation (GHMC) elections as the next major battleground, with all parties already initiating grassroots campaigns and strategy meetings. Discussions are also rife about a possible cabinet reshuffle to inject new vigor into the administration, particularly in light of recent public opinion surveys.

### 2. Law & Order

Telangana Police has significantly intensified its efforts against cybercrime and narcotics trafficking with the launch of "Operation Eagle Eye." This multi-pronged initiative has led to a series of coordinated raids across Hyderabad, Warangal, and Karimnagar, resulting in the arrest of several key figures involved in international drug rings and sophisticated online fraud schemes. Director General of Police (DGP) emphasized the department's commitment to leveraging technology for crime prevention and fostering stronger community policing relationships to tackle emerging threats.

### 3. Welfare

The state government presented a detailed progress report on its flagship welfare programs. The 'Mahalakshmi' scheme, offering free bus travel for women and a subsidy on domestic gas cylinders, reportedly reached over 80% of its target beneficiaries, though logistical challenges in remote areas were noted. Similarly, the 'Rythu Bharosa' financial assistance for farmers is nearing completion of its kharif installment distribution. In a major announcement, the government unveiled a new 'Yuva Kiranam' scheme, aiming to provide skill development and employment opportunities for unemployed youth across the state, with initial allocation of ₹500 crores.

### 4. Economy/IT

Hyderabad's standing as a global IT hub received another boost with the announcement by 'TechNova Inc.', a leading US-based technology firm, to establish a new state-of-the-art Research & Development (R&D) center in Gachibowli. This investment is projected to create over 5,000 high-skilled jobs in the next two years. The state's IT and ITeS exports witnessed a robust 15% increase in the last fiscal year, reaching a new milestone. The government also reiterated its commitment to promoting Tier-2 cities like Warangal and Karimnagar as emerging IT destinations.

### 5. Accidents

A tragic road accident occurred near Warangal, involving a private bus and a heavy goods vehicle, resulting in the loss of 12 lives and severe injuries to over 20 passengers. Preliminary reports suggest driver fatigue and overspeeding as potential causes. Chief Minister Revanth Reddy expressed profound grief, ordered a high-level inquiry into the incident, and announced an ex-gratia payment of ₹10 lakhs to the families of the deceased and comprehensive medical aid for the injured. Road safety campaigns are expected to be intensified in response.

### 6. Culture

Preparations are in full swing across Telangana for the upcoming Ugadi festival (Telugu New Year), with cultural organizations and government bodies organizing various events promoting Telugu traditions and arts. The Department of Language and Culture announced significant grants for traditional artists, folk performers, and craftsmen to preserve and promote Telangana's rich cultural heritage. Hyderabad is also currently hosting the "Global Telugu Film Festival," showcasing regional cinematic talent and attracting filmmakers and enthusiasts from across the globe.

### 7. Diplomatic

A high-level trade and investment delegation from Germany concluded a two-day visit to Hyderabad, expressing keen interest in collaborating with Telangana across sectors such as renewable energy, advanced manufacturing, and automotive industries. During their visit, a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) was signed between the Telangana Academy for Skill and Knowledge (TASK) and a German vocational training institute, focusing on skill development programs aligned with industry demands. The German Consul General highlighted Hyderabad's favorable investment climate.

### 8. Health/Education

In a significant push for rural healthcare, the state Health Minister inaugurated three new government medical colleges in Nagarkurnool, Sangareddy, and Mahabubabad districts, aimed at addressing the shortage of medical professionals and providing accessible healthcare. The 'Aarogyasri' health scheme's services were also expanded to include more critical illnesses and procedures. In education, the department is prioritizing digital literacy initiatives in government schools, especially in tribal and remote areas, to bridge the technological divide among students.

### 9. Infrastructure

The Hyderabad Metro Rail Limited (HMRL) has submitted the Detailed Project Report (DPR) for its ambitious Phase III expansion to the state government. The report outlines new corridors connecting key economic hubs like Gachibowli to the Outer Ring Road (ORR) and extending connectivity to high-growth residential areas. In other developments, a new multi-level flyover on the extended stretch of the PVNR Expressway was officially opened to the public, significantly easing traffic congestion and reducing commute times for residents heading towards Rajiv Gandhi International Airport.

### 10. Public Opinion

A recent independent survey conducted across Telangana revealed mixed public reactions to the current government's performance. While welfare schemes received moderate to high approval, concerns were raised regarding employment generation, increasing cost of living, and the pace of urban infrastructure development outside of Hyderabad's core areas. Social media platforms continue to be a significant forum for public discourse, with ongoing debates on issues ranging from water supply challenges in certain localities to the efficacy of agricultural policies.
